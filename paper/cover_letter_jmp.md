Dear Editor,

Please find enclosed our manuscript entitled "Spectral reflection symmetry and periodic multiplicity structure for quantum rotors on SO(3)/H", which we submit for consideration for publication in the Journal of Mathematical Physics.

The paper establishes a triple equivalence for quantum rigid rotors on quotient spaces SO(3)/H, where H is any finite subgroup of SO(3) with even order. We prove that three conditions are equivalent: (A) the commutator obstruction -1 in [2H, 2H], (B) a divisibility condition on element orders, and (C) a spectral reflection identity m(chi, j) + m(chi, j*-j) = 1 holding for every scalar superselection sector. While individual multiplicities and branching rules are classical, the global spectral structure --- reflection symmetry, binary occupation, periodic staircase --- has not been characterised previously. The equivalence (B) if and only if (C) shows that spectral structure is controlled by the 2-adic arithmetic of the group, and connects to the McKay correspondence through the invariant ring.

The paper also analyses symmetry breaking: when the rotation group is lowered from H to a subgroup K, spinorial sectors reappear if and only if Q8 is lost from the binary cover. For odd dihedral groups, where the global reflection fails, we prove that it persists selectively in spinorial sectors due to spectral invisibility of half-turn classes.

All proofs are classification-free, relying on element orders and Sylow structure rather than enumeration of polyhedral types. The results are supported by a comprehensive computational test suite (70 files, 1740 tests) publicly available at https://github.com/alex-toader/st_spinorial_obstruction.

We believe the manuscript aligns well with the scope of JMP in its combination of representation theory, spectral analysis on homogeneous spaces, and quantum mechanics on multiply connected configuration spaces --- topics with a long tradition in the journal (Schulman 1968, Finkelstein-Rubinstein 1968, Imbo-Sudarshan 1988).

This work is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. The author declares no conflict of interest.
